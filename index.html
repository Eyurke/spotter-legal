Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 7, 2026 · Effective: June 7, 2026

Spotter (“Spotter,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) provides a mobile gym‑tracking application that lets you log workouts, earn ranks, track personal records, and share progress with friends (the “App”). This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, how we use and share it, how long we keep it, and the rights and choices you have.

By creating an account or using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Who is responsible for your data

Spotter is the data controller responsible for the personal information described in this policy.

Contact for privacy requests: evgenyyurkevich40@gmail.com

If you contact us about a privacy request, we may ask you to verify your identity (for example, by confirming the email address associated with your account) before we act on the request.

2. Information we collect

We only collect the information needed to run the App. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use third‑party advertising networks or behavioral‑advertising trackers.

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account / contact information — your email address. Spotter uses passwordless email sign‑in: we send a one‑time code to your email to verify it. We do not collect or store a password.
  • Profile information — your chosen username and display name, and an optional profile photo.
  • Fitness profile — your body weight and the gender you select. These are used to calculate strength scores, ranks, percentiles, and personalized goals. Because this relates to your physical characteristics, it may be treated as health‑related (“special category” / sensitive) data in some jurisdictions; we process it only to provide the App’s core ranking and goal features.
  • Workout data — exercises, sets, weights, reps, durations, distances, personal records, ranks, training plans, and related notes you log.
  • Social content — posts, status/goal text, comments, “hype” reactions, friend requests, and friendships you create.
  • Photos — photos you attach to a workout or use as a profile picture. The App requests access to your camera and photo library only when you choose to add a photo. We do not access your photo library or camera in the background.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Push notification token — if you enable push notifications, your device’s push token is stored so we can deliver notifications (e.g., a friend’s reaction, comment, friend request, or duel update). You can disable this at any time in your device settings.
  • Technical/diagnostic data — basic technical information necessary to operate the service (such as your device type/platform for delivering push notifications correctly). We do not embed third‑party analytics or advertising SDKs in the App.

2.3 Information we do not collect

  • We do not collect precise location/GPS data. There is no gym‑location or location‑based feature in the App.
  • We do not use advertising identifiers or sell data to advertisers.
  • We do not collect payment information (the App is distributed through the Apple App Store / Google Play, which handle any purchases under their own policies).

3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Create and maintain your account and verify your identity at sign‑in.
  • Provide core features: logging workouts, calculating personal records, strength scores, ranks, percentiles, XP/levels, quests, leaderboards, and personalized goals.
  • Power social features: friends, friend requests, the activity feed, comments, hypes, notifications, and friend suggestions.
  • Send push and in‑app notifications you have enabled.
  • Maintain the security and integrity of the service and prevent abuse.
  • Comply with legal obligations and respond to your privacy requests.

4. Legal bases for processing (EEA/UK – GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract — to provide the App and the features you request.
  • Consent — for processing your fitness profile (body weight and gender) for ranking and personalization, for accessing your camera/photo library, and for sending push notifications. You may withdraw consent at any time (for example, by clearing those fields, revoking the permission in device settings, or deleting your account).
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the service secure, prevent abuse, and improve reliability, balanced against your rights.
  • Legal obligation — where we must process data to comply with applicable law.

5. How your information is shared

We share information only as described below.

5.1 With other users

The following are visible to other users by design:

  • Your username, display name, and profile photo.
  • Your ranks, personal records, and strength percentiles (visible on your public profile and on leaderboards).
  • Content you choose to share to the activity feed (workout summaries, text/goal posts, milestones), and your comments and hype reactions.
  • Friend requests and friendships.

Sharing a workout to the feed is opt‑in — nothing is posted to the feed unless you turn on the “Share to feed” toggle. Your raw email address and one‑time codes are never shown to other users.

5.2 With service providers (processors)

We use the following providers to operate the App. They process data on our behalf under their own security and privacy commitments:

  • Supabase — hosts our database and provides authentication (account creation, email one‑time codes) and storage. Your account, profile, workout, and social data are stored here.
  • Expo (Expo Application Services) — delivers push notifications via the Expo push service (which in turn uses Apple Push Notification service and/or Firebase Cloud Messaging) when you enable notifications.
  • Apple App Store / Google Play — distribute the App and handle app delivery and any in‑store transactions under their own privacy policies.

These providers may process and store data outside your country, including in the United States. Where required, such transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses).

5.3 Legal and safety disclosures

We may disclose information if required by law, to comply with legal process, to enforce our terms, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of our users, the public, or Spotter.

5.4 No sale of personal information

We do not sell your personal information and do not share it for cross‑context behavioral advertising.

6. Data retention

We keep your personal information for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, we delete or anonymize your personal information within a reasonable period, except where we must retain certain data to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce our agreements. Content you shared publicly (such as feed posts and comments) is removed when you delete your account or the relevant content.

7. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction — correct inaccurate or incomplete information (you can edit much of this directly in the App’s profile settings).
  • Deletion — request deletion of your account and personal information.
  • Restriction / objection — restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Portability — receive your data in a portable format.
  • Withdraw consent — withdraw consent at any time where we rely on it (this does not affect processing already carried out).

To exercise any of these rights, email evgenyyurkevich40@gmail.com. We will respond within the time required by applicable law. You will not be discriminated against for exercising your rights.

You can delete your account and associated data from within the App, or by emailing us. This removes your profile, workout logs, personal records, ranks, social posts, and other personal data associated with your account, subject to the retention exceptions in Section 6.
